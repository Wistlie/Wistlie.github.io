Personal Hobbies
Perhaps the thing that has driven me to study computer science has been my dedication to melee. Every since I was a kid, playing melee has been the funnest game I've ever played. For those that don't know, melee is a fighting game starring some famous nintendo characters. Ever since it's release, the scene for the game has been exponentially growing, with fans creating new ways to play and win the game by analyzing the game down to the frame-by-frame data. I was inspired by the dedication, and have joined the fanbase to hopefully one day contribute my coding skills and help develope future melee projects.
Along with melee, I have found utmost joy playing Quidditch. No, not the flying around by broomstick-in-the-air-version of Quidditch silly, I mean the run-on-land-with-a-broom-stuck-between-your-legs Quidditch we magicless muggles play. I play as a beater, who in the game is a person who throws balls called bludgers at players to make them temporarily leave the game. This gives my teammates opprotunity to score! Teamwork is essential in this game by the way, since more coordinated teams that blend their players together tend to win more games.
